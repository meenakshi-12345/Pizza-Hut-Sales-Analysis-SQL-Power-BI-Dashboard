# 🍕 Pizza Hut Sales Analysis | SQL + Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end sales analysis for a Pizza Hut business using **SQL** for data analysis and **Power BI** for interactive dashboard development.

The objective was not only to visualize sales performance but also to derive meaningful business insights and provide actionable recommendations to support decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

The business wanted to understand:

- Which pizza categories generate the highest revenue?
- Which pizzas perform best and worst?
- Which pizza sizes contribute the most revenue?
- Which weekdays and months generate the highest orders?
- Which products are popular but underperform in revenue?
- What business strategies can improve sales and profitability?

# 🔍 Key Business Insights

### 📈 Overview

- Classic pizzas generate the highest revenue and sales volume.
- Friday records the highest customer orders.
- July experiences peak sales while August–October shows declining demand.
- Large-sized pizzas contribute the highest revenue.

### 💰 Revenue Analysis

- Top-performing pizzas consistently generate over **35K** revenue.
- Bottom-performing pizzas contribute significantly less revenue, indicating opportunities for optimization.
- A considerable revenue gap exists between the highest and lowest-performing products.

### ⭐ Product Performance

- Thai Chicken, BBQ Chicken, and Classic Deluxe are consistent top performers across multiple KPIs.
- California Chicken and Spicy Italian generate high revenue despite fewer purchases.
- Hawaiian and Pepperoni have high demand but relatively lower revenue, suggesting opportunities for upselling and bundling.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

- Continue promoting top-performing pizzas through featured campaigns.
- Introduce combo meals using high-performing and low-performing pizzas.
- Improve menu visibility for premium pizzas.
- Increase Average Order Value through premium toppings and meal bundles.
- Investigate seasonal demand fluctuations between August and October.
- Monitor Revenue, Orders, and Quantity together before making pricing decisions.
